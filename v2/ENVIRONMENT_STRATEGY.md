# V2 Environment And Render Presentation Strategy

This strategy defines how environment, lighting, camera, stage, and real-time viewer details should support Lesly's 2026 senior-level 3D Character Artist portfolio.

## Core Principle

Lesly's portfolio is character-first. Environment detail should help the viewer inspect the character, believe the scale, read the materials, and feel the story. It should not reposition Lesly as an environment artist unless real environment work is approved and documented.

## Top-Tier 2026 Presentation Standard

Each real flagship character should eventually have two render modes.

### Beauty Stage

Purpose: make the character memorable in the first five seconds.

Required qualities:

- Character-dominant composition.
- Readable silhouette at thumbnail size.
- Grounded floor plane, plinth, or contact shadow.
- Controlled background with no visual noise behind the face, hands, outfit, or accessories.
- Key/fill/rim or HDRI-based lighting that shows form and material response.
- Camera angle that supports the character's personality without hiding anatomy or construction.
- Closeups for face, hair, hands, outfit, accessory, and material detail when real.

### Technical Stage

Purpose: let recruiters and art directors inspect the work.

Required qualities:

- Neutral background.
- Consistent lighting.
- Front/side/back or turntable/turnaround when available.
- Clay or material check render.
- Wireframe/topology, UV layout, texture/material sheet, bake maps, and export notes when real.
- No dramatic color grade that hides weak forms, bad bakes, missing detail, or topology issues.

## Environment Detail Rules

Use:

- Simple studio floor, plinth, base, or pedestal.
- Contact shadows and grounded feet.
- One or two story/scale props when they clarify the character.
- Clean backdrop or subtle set wall.
- Viewer/engine capture only when the source is real and named accurately.

Avoid:

- Full dioramas that imply environment art ownership.
- Heavy scenery that competes with the character.
- Generated background detail presented as real modeling, lighting, level design, or engine proof.
- Uncredited HDRIs, kitbash props, stock set dressing, or borrowed environment assets.
- Tool labels such as Unreal, Unity, Sketchfab, Marmoset, or Blender unless the capture/setup is real.

## Case Study Environment Slots

For a real Lesly project, collect these if available:

| Slot | Target filename | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Beauty stage | `beauty-stage.webp` | Cinematic character-first render |
| Neutral stage | `neutral-stage.webp` | Inspection-friendly render |
| Viewer/engine capture | `viewer-engine.webp` | Real Sketchfab, Marmoset, Unity, or Unreal proof |
| Environment notes | `environment-notes.webp` | Lighting, camera, HDRI, prop, and stage notes |

These are optional additions to the required case-study files in `LAUNCH_CHECKLIST.md`. They should never replace topology, UV, texture, sculpt, or deliverables proof.

## Copy Rules

Safe wording:

- "Studio-stage beauty render."
- "Neutral inspection render."
- "Character presentation stage."
- "Simple grounded scene for scale and material read."
- "Viewer capture, if verified."

Unsafe wording unless verified:

- "Environment art."
- "Unreal scene."
- "Unity-ready scene."
- "Marmoset render setup."
- "Sketchfab model viewer."
- "Production lighting."
- "Delivered environment."

## Current V2 Status

The current V2 generated visuals use studio-style presentation and simple character staging. They are approved only as generated/site-created concept visuals. They do not prove real Lesly environment art, lighting setup, render scene, engine integration, or viewer setup.
