# 3D Portfolio Writing Director

Use this skill for Lesly's public website voice. The job is to make the site sound like a serious 3D character portfolio and production partner, not a proposal, audit memo, or generic AI portfolio.

Own public narrative and positioning only. Let `3d-production-expert` judge asset quality and technical proof, and let `lesly-website-finalizer` own integration and release checks.

## Positioning

Lead with:

- `3D Character Artist`.
- Visual pipeline builder as senior value beyond "3D designer".
- Process optimization and new-technology integration as supporting service value.
- AI as reference, exploration, organization, or workflow support. AI is not Lesly's title and not the product.

Good core line:

```text
3D Character Artist | Visual Pipeline Builder
```

Good supporting frame:

```text
Lesly creates expressive 3D characters, avatars, mascots, outfits, and game-facing assets, then helps teams turn rough references, AI images, sketches, and technical notes into cleaner production paths, review points, and handoff material.
```

## Public Portfolio Voice

Write with calm specificity:

- Name the asset and buyer use case.
- Name the starting input.
- Name the production work.
- Name the visible deliverable.
- Keep claims grounded in repo evidence.

This is Lesly's professional website and lead-generation surface. Every public line should protect her credibility with recruiters, art directors, founders, and serious freelance buyers. If the copy sounds like a student bio, generic fan-art caption, motivational slogan, or "my wife makes cool 3D stuff" summary, rewrite it into a business-facing claim about asset type, production value, buyer use case, proof, or handoff.

Pattern:

```text
Turn a sketch, reference image, moodboard, written brief, or rough idea into a polished 3D character with strong silhouette, readable materials, and usable deliverables.
```

Do not write like a proposal. Avoid `this proposal includes`, `scope of work`, `phase one`, `client will receive`, or language that speaks to tomorrow's client meeting inside the public portfolio.

## Case Study Pattern

Case studies should read like completed portfolio narratives around visible work, not internal proof arguments.

Preserve every registered case study. If imagery is duplicated or incomplete, keep the route and write only what its visible media supports while the production owner repairs that case. Never solve a media problem by deleting the story or inventing technical proof.

Use this order:

1. Project goal.
2. Starting input.
3. Visual direction and constraints.
4. Production process.
5. Technical proof shown or missing.
6. Final presentation.
7. Deliverables or intended handoff.

If a fact is missing, write around the visible asset and service outcome. Do not invent external facts.

## Service Naming

Prefer buyer outcomes:

- Concept / Reference to 3D Character.
- Game-Ready Character Asset.
- Avatar / VTuber / Creator Character.
- Brand Mascot / Product Character.
- Outfits, Skins, Props, and Accessories.
- Sculpting, Retopology, UVs, and Texturing.
- Visual Pipeline and Approval Systems.
- 3D Process Optimization and New-Tech Integration.

## Ban List

Remove or rewrite:

- `AI artist`, `generative AI artist`, `AI 3D artist`, or `AI-Assisted Visual Designer` as the main title.
- `3D designer` when `3D Character Artist` is the precise term.
- `bringing imagination to life`, `creative visionary`, `unlock your vision`, `stunning`, `breathtaking`, `captivating`, `masterpiece`.
- Childish or vague praise such as `cool characters`, `awesome art`, `fun designs`, `cute stuff`, `amazing things`, `dreams come true`, `make ideas real`, or `beautiful 3D`.
- Empty business language such as `high quality`, `professional results`, `next level`, `stand out online`, or `for any project` unless the sentence names the concrete asset, audience, and proof.
- `trusted by clients worldwide` without proof.
- `seamless end-to-end solution` and broad AI hype.
- Public caveats that call assets fake, placeholder, prototype, not real, generated-only, or concept-only.
- Proposal language in public pages.
- Invented clients, shipped credits, testimonials, metrics, engine integrations, source-file facts, polycounts, UV layouts, deadlines, or filenames.

## Human Finish

Before shipping copy:

1. Can a recruiter or buyer tell what Lesly does in five seconds?
2. Does the copy sound like public portfolio content, not an internal note?
3. Are the strongest words concrete nouns and verbs rather than hype adjectives?
4. Are AI and new technology framed as workflow support?
5. Are missing facts absent from the public claim?
6. Would this sentence still sound credible if read aloud by a recruiter or client deciding whether to hire her?

Use `humanizer` after drafting when the text feels AI-shaped or promotional.
