## Universal Rules

- Make one case answer one main professional question.
- Lead the public story with the artwork, artistic premise, supported role, and strongest representative result. Keep status, source, and evidence classification internal unless they are naturally relevant public facts.
- Include only proof that supports the title and comes from the matching asset or record.
- Use a documented constraint, decision, and consequence when a real decision record exists. Otherwise discuss a visible artistic rationale tied to approved media. Never invent chronology or a production decision to make the case feel senior.
- Separate authored work, inherited inputs, collaborators, reviewers, vendors, and downstream owners.
- Close the public story with a supportable finished presentation or documented outcome. Keep unresolved states and missing-proof notes in the internal truth guard.
- Use generated images as direction or presentation evidence only.
- Hard blockers remove or narrow only the affected public claims. They do not block discussion of supported artistic decisions, and they never become public warnings, evidence-status labels, or capture requests.

Every proof sequence below is an internal guide for evaluating a matching technical or outcome claim. A case may pass without completing the sequence when its title and narrative stay within the visible artistic scope.

## Character And Game

**Professional question**

Can Lesly preserve identity, anatomy, costume logic, prop interaction, movement needs, and target constraints through a character-production problem?

**Proof sequence**

1. Permissioned brief, concept, base, or starting input.
2. Identity-led beauty result.
3. Neutral or coordinated views that expose anatomy, silhouette, costume, and prop logic.
4. One meaningful form, construction, movement, or target-platform decision.
5. Matching technical evidence proportional to the title.
6. Final result in its verified target context.

**Capture real**

- source sculpt/model and matching high/low;
- readable topology and deformation loops;
- UVs, bakes, texture channels, shaders, and material setup;
- rig, blendshapes, visemes, tracking, deformation, prop interaction;
- engine, viewer, export, skeleton, counts, texture scope, or performance facts.

**Generate presentation**

- identity hero;
- coordinated visual-direction views;
- costume or prop construction direction;
- movement-risk or deformation-target direction;
- material, lighting, stage, or thumbnail-read presentation.

**Hard blockers**

- no `game-ready`, retopology, PBR, rig-ready, avatar-ready, engine, export, count, or measured specification without matching evidence;
- no faceted clay image described as low-poly topology;
- no generated expression sheet described as a working facial system;
- no unrelated setting detail presented as Lesly's environment ownership.

## Props And Interiors

**Professional question**

Can Lesly build or direct a coherent asset system with believable scale, construction, material language, viewing-distance priorities, and scene logic?

**Proof sequence**

1. Brief/reference and intended use.
2. Blockout, scale, or hierarchy decision.
3. Asset-family, modular, or construction logic.
4. Matching material and technical proof.
5. Final object family or scene in context.

**Capture real**

- object hierarchy, named assets, source scene, and scale;
- matching models, high/low, wireframe, UVs, maps, and shaders;
- modular system, pivots, functional construction, collision, LOD, performance, engine, or exports;
- measured dimensions or production specifications.

**Generate presentation**

- hero prop or set-dressing direction;
- coherent asset-family lineup;
- visual scale relationship using a non-technical approved reference;
- material hierarchy or lighting direction;
- composition, contact, or viewing-distance comparison.

**Hard blockers**

- no production-environment, modular, optimized, game-ready, functional-hardware, or engine claim from a polished shelf or room image;
- no generated lineup described as matching production assets;
- no decorative material board treated as UV, map, shader, or PBR evidence.

## Visual Direction And Client Review

**Professional question**

Can Lesly turn ambiguous input and accountable human feedback into a clear decision and usable handoff without overstating ownership or approval?

**Proof sequence**

1. Real or explicitly fictional/reconstructed input and status.
2. Genuinely different options.
3. Constraint, reviewer, feedback, or reconstructed selection criteria.
4. Selected direction and rationale.
5. Handoff artifact and downstream state.
6. A documented downstream state when one exists; otherwise close on the supportable visual result without inventing an outcome.

**Capture real**

- permissioned inputs, comments, drawovers, versions, dates, reviewer/decision owner, and approval state;
- real change record, delivery structure, handoff, implementation, or downstream result;
- site captures, locations, responsible reviewers, measurements, decisions, follow-up, and closure for construction/progress work.

**Generate presentation**

- explicitly reconstructed direction options;
- labelled finish, camera, staging, or identity comparisons;
- proposed review criteria or handoff requirements expressed through images plus semantic HTML/captions;
- evidence hierarchy or risk direction that does not impersonate an operated workflow.

**Hard blockers**

- no client, approval, implementation, construction, site-verification, measured-outcome, or operational-pipeline language without a real record;
- no generated comments, dates, status tokens, UI, dashboards, portals, or approval trails;
- no AI headline that displaces Lesly's human-owned visual decision role.

## Print And Collectible

**Professional question**

Can Lesly preserve character or prop appeal while addressing the real constraints of a stable, printable, assemblable physical object?

**Proof sequence**

1. Concept, scope, intended scale, and responsibility split.
2. Digital form and silhouette decision.
3. Engineering risks and matching part strategy.
4. Slicer/support/test evidence.
5. Physical assembly, finish, and credited result.

**Capture real**

- source geometry, actual cuts, keyed joints, tolerances, dimensions, minimum thickness, watertightness, hollowing, and drainage;
- supports, slicer, settings, failed/revised tests, repairs, fit, assembly, and physical print;
- printer, engineer, assembler, painter, and photographer credits.

**Generate presentation**

- collectible beauty or clay/form direction;
- multiple coordinated views for silhouette and contact risks;
- explicitly proposed component grouping;
- balance, base-contact, fragile-form, cape, tail, staff, or ornament risk direction.

**Hard blockers**

- no print-ready, manufacturable, production-part, tested-setting, successful-assembly, or physical-output claim from generated media;
- no proposed separation described as final cuts, keys, tolerances, or engineered geometry;
- no generated slicer screen, test print, product photo, or shipping outcome.

## Ranked Next-Capture Logic — Internal Only

This backlog never enters the public story packet, captions, metadata, or page sections.

Choose one next capture using this order:

1. Remove the highest-risk unsupported public title or claim.
2. Answer the case's main professional question with matching evidence.
3. Add missing proof to a flagship or underrepresented portfolio category.
4. Prefer an available matching source with known ownership and permission.
5. Increase differentiation from the other registered cases.
6. Use effort or convenience only after the evidence priorities above.

Record one primary capture. Keep secondary candidates in the evidence backlog, not repeated public copy.
