# Humanizer

Polish approved portfolio copy without changing its meaning, evidence, or commercial intent.

## Ownership boundary

- Let `3d-portfolio-writing-director` decide positioning, narrative, and service framing.
- Let `3d-production-expert` decide what visual and technical claims the evidence supports.
- Use this skill only for the final language pass.

## Editing method

1. Enforce the public/internal and truth boundaries before preserving prose. Reject internal-review language and irrelevant website-production provenance even when the words are factually true.
2. Preserve approved public facts and intended meaning, not a mandatory paragraph shape, facts grid, or repeated scaffold.
3. Remove generic praise, vague significance, repetitive framing, filler, and chatbot correspondence.
4. Prefer concrete nouns and active verbs over promotional adjectives.
5. Vary sentence rhythm naturally. Keep useful quirks and specific details.
6. For case studies, use first-person singular with a calm, observant artist voice: specific about form and decisions, modest about unsupported history, and reflective without sounding academic.
7. Read the result aloud mentally and remove phrases that sound like a proposal, audit, internal agent note, or description of building the portfolio page.
8. Reject rather than polish any public sentence that exposes missing proof, capture requests, claim states, hypothetical production instructions, approval/gate state, or other internal truth-guard content.
9. Compare the case with the rest of the public corpus and rewrite repeated openings, transitions, three-part lists, conclusions, and website-authorship formulas.

Common problems include:

- `bringing imagination to life`, `creative visionary`, `stunning`, or `breathtaking`;
- abstract AI vocabulary such as `delve`, `tapestry`, `pivotal`, or `unlock`;
- forced groups of three, excessive headings, repetitive conclusions, or fake enthusiasm;
- vague claims attributed to unnamed experts, clients, or industry opinion;
- invented personality, biography, production facts, or outcomes used to fill a gap.
- `Portfolio case author`, `Zero-to-one website presentation`, `website case`, media-order narration, or similar self-referential scaffolding used in place of the artistic story.

Do not ban punctuation mechanically. An em dash, semicolon, transition, or passive construction is acceptable when it suits the voice and improves the sentence. Judge clusters and context instead of treating one surface feature as proof of AI writing.

## Output check

Before returning polished copy, confirm:

- approved meaning, facts, and evidence boundaries are unchanged;
- no project, client, result, metric, tool, or deliverable was invented;
- the copy still covers every required narrative function without preserving a rigid template;
- it sounds like Lesly's professional portfolio rather than a chatbot response.
- the first-person voice, opening, and conclusion are not copied from another case.
