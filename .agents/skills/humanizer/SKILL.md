# Humanizer

Polish approved portfolio copy without changing its meaning, evidence, or commercial intent.

## Ownership boundary

- Let `3d-portfolio-writing-director` decide positioning, narrative, and service framing.
- Let `3d-production-expert` decide what visual and technical claims the evidence supports.
- Use this skill only for the final language pass.

## Editing method

1. Preserve every factual claim, limitation, and required paragraph.
2. Remove generic praise, vague significance, repetitive framing, filler, and chatbot correspondence.
3. Prefer concrete nouns and active verbs over promotional adjectives.
4. Vary sentence rhythm naturally. Keep useful quirks and specific details.
5. Match an available Lesly voice sample; otherwise use calm, direct professional English.
6. Read the result aloud mentally and remove phrases that sound like a proposal, audit, or internal agent note.

Common problems include:

- `bringing imagination to life`, `creative visionary`, `stunning`, or `breathtaking`;
- abstract AI vocabulary such as `delve`, `tapestry`, `pivotal`, or `unlock`;
- forced groups of three, excessive headings, repetitive conclusions, or fake enthusiasm;
- vague claims attributed to unnamed experts, clients, or industry opinion;
- invented personality, biography, production facts, or outcomes used to fill a gap.

Do not ban punctuation mechanically. An em dash, semicolon, transition, or passive construction is acceptable when it suits the voice and improves the sentence. Judge clusters and context instead of treating one surface feature as proof of AI writing.

## Output check

Before returning polished copy, confirm:

- meaning and evidence boundaries are unchanged;
- no project, client, result, metric, tool, or deliverable was invented;
- the copy still covers every required point;
- it sounds like Lesly's professional portfolio rather than a chatbot response.
