# Lesly Final Content Strategy

This is the working content strategy for finalizing the final site before hosting.

## Executive Recommendation

Position Lesly as a specialist:

> 3D Character Artist creating production-ready characters from sketches, moodboards, written briefs, AI references, and existing concepts.

Use AI as workflow support, not identity. The strongest client-facing promise is:

> From concept or AI reference to a polished 3D character with usable production deliverables.

Do not lead with "AI artist," "AI-Assisted Visual Designer," or broad "3D designer." Those make the offer less specific and weaker for game studios and serious clients.

## Pricing And Hourly Rate

Recommendation: do not put a large "$100/hr" label in the hero or nav.

Why:

- A public hourly rate can make clients compare hours instead of outcomes.
- Character work varies heavily by complexity, deliverables, revision cycles, and technical requirements.
- Project pricing is easier for non-technical clients to understand.
- Full-time recruiters do not need to see freelance hourly pricing above the fold.

What to show instead:

- Use budget ranges in the project form.
- Add "Projects quoted by scope" near the services section.
- Optionally add "Limited hourly consulting available from $100/hr" only under consulting or art-direction support.

Recommended visible pricing language:

> Projects are quoted by scope, complexity, timeline, and deliverables. Small assets and consultation can start lower; complete character packages are scoped after reviewing references and technical requirements.

Recommended form budget ranges:

## Environment And Render Presentation Strategy

Use environment detail as a senior presentation layer around the character, not as a second discipline. The final site should look like Lesly understands camera, lighting, scale, story, and material response, while keeping the portfolio unmistakably character-first.

### 2026 top-tier target

Each real flagship character should eventually have two presentation modes:

1. **Beauty stage**
   - Purpose: make the character memorable in the first five seconds.
   - Visual language: grounded floor plane, soft contact shadow, controlled backdrop, readable silhouette, key/fill/rim or HDRI-based lighting, tasteful camera angle, and minimal context props.
   - Rule: the environment should support face, silhouette, hands, outfit, material variation, and story. It should not become a diorama unless Lesly made the diorama.

2. **Technical stage**
   - Purpose: let a recruiter or art director inspect the work.
   - Visual language: neutral background, consistent lighting, full-body front/side/back or turntable, clay/material views, wireframe/topology, UVs, texture/material sheets, and export notes where real.
   - Rule: dramatic lighting cannot hide weak forms, muddy materials, bad bakes, missing hands/feet, or topology gaps.

### Environment detail to show

- Ground contact: contact shadow, floor plane, plinth, or simple stage so the model does not float.
- Scale cue: one restrained prop, base, weapon, accessory, UI ruler, or turnaround grid only if it clarifies the character.
- Lighting plan: key, fill, rim, HDRI, or neutral inspection lighting, with notes if the capture is real.
- Camera plan: one hero angle, one full-body neutral, closeups for face/hair/hands/accessories, and turnarounds.
- Material response: light the character so skin, fabric, leather, metal, hair, emissive, and painted surfaces read clearly.
- Real-time proof: if using Sketchfab, Marmoset Viewer, Unity, or Unreal, label the viewer/engine and keep settings honest.

### What not to imply

- Do not claim environment art, level design, cinematic rendering, Unreal integration, Unity integration, Marmoset scene setup, or Sketchfab viewer proof unless those files/captures are real.
- Do not use generated background detail as proof of modeling, set dressing, lighting setup, engine setup, or delivered environment work.
- Do not bury the character in scenery. If a thumbnail is more about the set than the character, it is wrong for this portfolio.

### Current final state

The generated site concept visuals already follow a simple studio-stage direction. They can support the site as generated concept presentation, but they are not proof of Lesly's real lighting setup, render scene, engine scene, or environment art. Future real assets should replace them with beauty-stage and technical-stage evidence.
