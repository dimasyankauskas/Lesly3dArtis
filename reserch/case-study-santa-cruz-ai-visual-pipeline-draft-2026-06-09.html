The problem

The team had AI outputs, but not a production workflow.

Cost drift

Cloud credits kept burning.

Online AI tools were being used repeatedly to chase acceptable images. Monthly spend reached an estimated $2,000, but the team still lacked a stable path from reference to client-ready visuals.

Consistency failure

Materials and geometry changed between runs.

Prompts alone did not preserve house shape, site context, finish selections, siding, tile, fixtures, or the builder's preferred presentation style.

Communication gap

Client review lived in scattered channels.

Photos, client comments, voice notes, agency replies, and design options were split across texts, calls, emails, folders, and disconnected AI outputs.

Lesly's role

She defined the visual pipeline, then helped the technical team implement it.

What Lesly owned

  • Art-direction standards for construction visualization.
  • Material and design-library structure for repeatable output.
  • Prompt, reference, seed, and review discipline for local generation.
  • Workflow requirements for client portal comments, photo review, and agency response.
  • Training plan for a work-in-progress agentic AI and 3D workflow.

What developers / agency implemented

  • Local Stable Diffusion / ComfyUI generation environment.
  • Apple Silicon workstation setup for private visual iteration.
  • MCP-style agent connections to 3D tools where feasible.
  • Internal workflow surfaces for photos, comments, summaries, and client status.
  • Tooling glue, automation, security, permissions, and deployment.
Material library

Siding, stone, tile, wood, paint, fixtures, landscape, lighting mood.

Local generation

ComfyUI graph, ControlNet guidance, seed logs, reusable prompt blocks.

3D / MCP agents

Blender/3D tool connection for scene checks, asset notes, and guided setup.

Client portal

Progress photos, comments, audio/text notes, agency replies, approvals.

Workflow architecture

The final workflow made visual decisions traceable.

Brief intake

Collect site photos, client notes, plans, material selections, and desired visual outcome.

Direction board

Lesly defines visual target, finish options, prompt blocks, and consistency rules.

Local generation

Generate controlled variants using local Stable Diffusion/ComfyUI workflows and structure guidance.

3D/MCP review

Use agentic tooling where useful to connect notes, references, and 3D application checks.

Client portal

Publish progress visuals, accept comments, route responses, and record decisions.
