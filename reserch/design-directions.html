Below are 4 fully-realized design directions for the Lesly 3D Character Artist site. Each one uses the same content brief, so the contrast is purely design and audience fit, not copy. Open this file in a browser and scroll. After each direction you'll find a score and a 2-line note about what the direction is for and what it trades off.

Two audiences are being weighed: hiring managers at US game studios who spend 6–15 seconds on a portfolio, and clients hiring an elite $100/hr freelance character artist who decide on taste, process visibility, and production maturity. A direction that scores 5/5 for one audience usually scores 2–3/5 for the other. Pick the one that matches where Lesly's next year is going.

Placeholder geometry stands in for the real portfolio images. When you pick a direction, those blocks are replaced with the actual mockup art from reserch/visuals/.

Direction 1
I.

Editorial Atelier

Gold-on-black, 3-column hero, all-caps condensed display + serif italics body. Fashion-magazine composition. The character is the brand, not a card with borders.

FT recruiter skim3 / 5Beautiful but skimmable only after the 1st scroll. 6-second filter is harder to pass.
Elite $100/hr freelance5 / 5"I'm a working artist, not a marketing site" — taste signal, no template aesthetic.

Why this works: art-direction circles share this aesthetic. The character is the entire brand.
What you trade: recruiters may bounce at the 1st screen thinking it's a portfolio-only site with no "production proof" density.

IV.

Quiet Premium

Cream, generous whitespace, type-driven, restrained. Cormorant Garamond italic display + Inter body. The aesthetic of a luxury craftsperson's site. No CTAs above the fold; conversion happens by trust, not buttons.

FT recruiter skim3 / 5Skimmable after the 1st scroll. 6-second filter is harder but not impossible — type carries it.

What you trade: recruiters used to dense production portfolios may under-read it. Slows the funnel, raises the floor.
