Executive summary

From scattered updates to a repeatable client review system.

The problem

The team had progress evidence, but not a review system.

Scattered communication

Client feedback lived everywhere.

Homeowner comments came through calls, texts, email, casual site conversations, shared folders, and image threads. Decisions were easy to lose and hard to audit later.

Inconsistent capture

Photos did not always answer the client's question.

Contractors were already taking photos, but there was no shared standard for angle, distance, milestone, material, issue, comparison, or next action.

AI visuals were disconnected

Generated options did not stay tied to construction reality.

AI-generated options helped with imagination, but they needed to be connected to actual jobsite progress, material selections, constraints, and client approvals.

Portal model

Lesly defined the review surface around visual evidence.

The core design decision: every update should connect a real photo, a status, a client comment, an owner/agency response, and a next action. AI supports summaries and routing; it does not replace human review.

Workflow architecture

The workflow made progress, comments, and visual decisions traceable.

Capture rule

Contractor captures photo, phase, issue type, material, and optional voice/video note.

AI intake

Agent summarizes notes, tags the update, drafts a progress description, and flags missing context.

Visual review

Lesly's standards map each image to client-ready visual quality and material consistency.

Client portal

Homeowner sees project phase, photos, status, options, comments, and next actions.

Agency response

Agency responds with text, audio summary, or controlled AI visual options where useful.

Decision log

Approved choices feed back into material library, project notes, and future visual prompts.
