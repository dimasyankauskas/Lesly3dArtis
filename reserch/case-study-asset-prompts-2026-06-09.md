## Global Prompt Rules

Use these rules in every image prompt:

- Match Lesly's current portfolio style: cinematic 3D character presentation, warm bronze/gold accents, dark neutral studio backgrounds, Belleza-era editorial framing, premium but not flashy.
- Make the asset feel like a real portfolio board, not a pitch deck or proposal page.
- Keep characters dominant. Environments support scale, lighting, and material read.
- Show process visually: rough input, blockout, sculpt/clay, material study, technical inspection, final render, deliverables.
- Avoid readable text inside generated images. Use blank label strips, small icon blocks, color swatches, thumbnail panels, arrows, and clean grid layout. The website captions will provide real text.
- Do not include fake logos, fake client names, fake UI screenshots, fake software screenshots, fake polycounts, fake dates, fake signatures, fake file names, or fake engine labels.
- Do not use watermarks, UI chrome, misspelled labels, lorem text, signatures, or artist names.
- Output target: 16:9 WebP-ready image, clean composition, no decorative clutter, no cropped-off important anatomy unless the shot is intentionally a detail crop.

## Global Negative Prompt

fake client logo, fake testimonial, fake readable text, misspelled labels, watermark, signature, QR code, UI screenshot, software menu, fake polycount, fake date, fake file name, generic AI art collage, blurry topology, broken hands, distorted face, unreadable silhouette, overbuilt environment, full level art scene, proposal deck, corporate slide, stock photo, flat infographic, random unrelated props

## Acceptance Review For Generated Images

Before adding any generated or upgraded image to the public site:

- Does it strengthen the specific case, or is it just decoration?
- Does it show a real process slot: input, direction, WIP, technical proof, final, or deliverables?
- Does it avoid fake readable labels and unsupported production facts?
- Are face, hands, anatomy, silhouette, materials, and crop acceptable?
- Does the page caption explain what the image proves without inventing tools or metrics?
