Approval Language

One page, three decision modes

Client View

“This is the mood, scale, finish direction, and intended presence of the bar cabinet in your home.”

  • Approve the overall visual direction.
  • React to blackened steel, dark wood, warm glass, and stone.
  • Confirm whether the visual direction matches the quality and setting of the space.

Studio View

“This visual explains the project clearly enough to reduce ambiguity before final detailing.”

  • Identify what still needs material or hardware confirmation.
  • Keep visual revisions separate from fabrication revisions.
  • Create reusable presentation assets for future clients.

Fabrication View

“This is not the replacement for shop drawings. It is the approval layer that supports them.”

  • Use final CAD/PDF dimensions before fabrication-accurate renders.
  • Label concept visuals separately from production visuals.
  • Keep exact drawings as the source of truth for build details.
Client Approval Checklist

What the client can approve today

  • Overall mood: refined private-bar atmosphere with handcrafted materiality.
  • General cabinet proportions, arched opening, and four closed metal-framed windows.
  • Material direction: dark stained wood, blackened steel, stone top, warm interior glow.
  • Presentation direction for the next technical fidelity pass.

Important: final fabrication dimensions, lattice decisions, hardware details, and finish samples still require verified technical approval.
