## Reject / do not use as final visual direction

- Do not use the exterior white bifold-window photo as the aesthetic direction. It is only a mechanism example.
- Do not assume the final colors are warm walnut, bronze, or blackened steel. Those are proposal mood directions until One Off confirms finishes.
- Do not force the lattice/diamond infill in generated visuals. The client said the lattice infill is not determined and can be left out for now.
- Do not claim the render is fabrication-accurate. The client said dimensions are reference and One Off will tell the final look.
- Do not reduce the proposal to the original narrow ask. The original ask was 3D rendering, but the client context supports a stronger approval-experience proposal.

## Updated working understanding

The object is a large custom cabinet / bar-window wall for a huge house in Montana. The cabinet sits at or against a thick wall/cove condition, with the window system located in that cove. The lower/tall windows operate as two sliding bifold pairs. The work is still at a reference/coordination stage for dimensions, final finishes, and lattice/infill. The proposal may responsibly offer more than a render, but the first deliverable should still prove the narrow ask: accurate visual understanding of the object, mechanism, and client-facing approval value.
