# 11 — Meeting Talk Track

## Opening
“Thank you for sharing the bar-window drawings. I reviewed the elevation, section, plan, and the notes about the concealed bifold track. My understanding is that this is not a simple cabinet-door problem; it is a custom operable bar-window system inside a larger built-in wall.”

## Strategic reframing
“I can help create the 3D visual, but I think the stronger opportunity is to create a client-facing visualization workflow. For high-value work, the client needs to understand the object, the mechanism, the material direction, and how the piece sits in the space before fabrication.”

## Technical credibility
“I understand the lower four panels as two bifold sliding pairs. The left pair folds and parks left; the right pair folds and parks right; the track is concealed at top and bottom. I would want to confirm the wall/cove depth and whether the area behind is a shallow display niche or a deeper back-bar condition.”

## Business value
“This can reduce misunderstanding, help the designer and client approve faster, and make the overall commission feel more premium.”

## Proposal
“I suggest starting with a focused pilot: one closed hero view, one correct open-state view, a mechanism explanation, and a private mobile presentation page. If that proves useful, we can turn it into a repeatable visualization and client-portal workflow for future projects.”
