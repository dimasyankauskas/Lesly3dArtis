## Correct mechanism interpretation
The lower bar-window system has exactly **four lower glass/metal panels**, grouped as **two bifold sliding assemblies**:

```text
CLOSED:
[ 1 ][ 2 ][ 3 ][ 4 ]

LEFT PAIR ONLY OPEN:
[ folded stack: 1+2 parked left ]    open left-center area    [ 3 ][ 4 closed ]

BOTH SIDES OPEN:
[ folded stack: 1+2 parked left ]    clear center opening    [ folded stack: 3+4 parked right ]
```

## Critical rules
- Do not show four independent swing doors.
- Do not show two separate open left doors.
- Do not show four panels folded to one side.
- Do not turn the opening into a walk-in room or kitchen.
- Do not make the lower window system look like normal cabinet doors.
- The folded pair should read as one compact bifold stack.

## Rail / hardware notes
Based on conversation notes and hardware photos:

- Windows slide on a linear rail.
- Shoulder bolt anchors the window to a bearing.
- Linear rail is planned at top and bottom.
- Rail may be buried/concealed inside square tube.
- Dimensions are reference/coordination; final fabrication solution may change.
- Client note says the four tall windows are planned as 3/4 x 1 1/2 tube.

## Depth interpretation
The render should show a **shallow-to-moderate recessed bar/display niche**, not a full room behind the window.

Recommended visual depth:

- client-message evidence says the wall/cove is understood as approximately 12 inches thick
- approximately 12 inches of visible cove depth as the current working assumption, unless One Off confirms otherwise
- close shelves behind the opening
- bottles/glassware on removable shelves
- finished wood/stone back panel if appropriate
- warm integrated light

Avoid:

- full back kitchen
- walk-in room
- visible floor behind the opening
- sink area unless explicitly confirmed
- deep counter and room-like perspective

## Corrected visual story
This is a built-in bar-wall system with an operable metal/glass bifold bar window inside a thick wall/cove condition. The visual should communicate architecture + furniture + mechanism.

## Finish / infill boundary

- The drawing labels a stained glass panel insert.
- The client-message intake says specific colors are not confirmed yet.
- The same intake says the lattice infill has not been determined and is OK to leave out for now.
- Therefore, treat stained/leaded/diamond glass as a drawing/reference design direction, not a confirmed final infill unless One Off confirms it.
