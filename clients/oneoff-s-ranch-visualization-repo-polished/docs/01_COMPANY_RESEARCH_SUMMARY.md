# 01 — Company Research Summary

## One Off Furniture public positioning
The public language around One Off Furniture supports a premium, craft-led, trade/design-collaboration positioning.

Key public themes gathered during research:

- Custom metalwork fabricated with attention to detail.
- Collaboration with designers and artisans.
- Custom furniture and architectural elements.
- Functional, beautiful objects.
- Traditional design sensibilities and methods combined with modern techniques and aesthetics.
- Each piece should be individual and in harmony with its environment.
- Trade-oriented project intake.

## Strategic implication
This is not a low-cost furniture visualization context. The company’s public language and project type suggest bespoke work, designer collaboration, and high-value architectural objects. The correct proposal should speak to:

- collaboration
- craft
- trust
- design intent
- client approval
- premium experience
- visualization as part of service quality

## Recommended wording
“Your drawings are essential for fabrication. My proposal is to add a client-facing visualization layer that helps designers and clients understand, approve, and emotionally connect with the work before fabrication.”

## Wording to avoid
- “Your workflow is old-school.”
- “I can automate this.”
- “I use AI to generate images.”
- “I can make a 3D object from a 2D file.”

The technology can exist behind the scenes; the business message should focus on client confidence, clarity, and premium experience.
