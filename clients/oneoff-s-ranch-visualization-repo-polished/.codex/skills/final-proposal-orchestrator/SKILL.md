# Skill: Final Proposal Orchestrator

Use this skill before creating final client-facing proposal pages, pitch copy, visual assets, business recommendations, or send packages for the One Off / S-RANCH work.

## Required sequence

Do not skip or reorder these gates:

1. `oneoff-context` — confirm One Off business context and premium client-experience framing.
2. `bar-window-technical-review` — confirm the 2+2 sliding bifold mechanism, cove depth, rail logic, and finish/infill boundaries.
3. `visualization-director` — keep Lesly positioned as Client Visualization & Experience Director.
4. `asset-generation-pipeline` — plan and QC AI stills before site integration.
5. `quality-control` — reject visual or copy failures before they reach the final page.
6. `mobile-presentation-design-system` — keep the proposal mobile-first and client-polished.
7. `private-commission-portal` — describe portal ideas without overpromising production systems.
8. `proposal-writer` — write the final message and client page copy only after the above checks pass.
9. `video-prompt-director` — use video only after stills are accepted.

## Client-send rules

- Send only the polished final client page and final message.
- Keep `site/alignment-questionnaire.html`, archived HTML pages, raw intake files, and internal audits out of the client send package.
- Use a focused **Visual Strategy Pilot** as the primary next-step concept.
- Do not include pricing, dollar ranges, package ladders, retainers, or estimate calculators in the client presentation unless the user explicitly reverses this decision.
- Keep pricing strategy internal until One Off shows interest and the user asks to prepare a separate commercial proposal.

## Stop rules

Stop before sending or generating final client assets if:

- the both-sides-open visual is missing or wrong,
- final colors or lattice/infill are stated as confirmed without One Off confirmation,
- the proposal sounds like it criticizes One Off's drawings or workflow,
- the presentation discusses pricing, dollar values, retainers, or estimate calculators,
- the page contains placeholder language,
- the page links to internal questionnaires, archives, raw intake, or audit docs,
- the page implies the portal is already production-connected to email, CRM, Slack, login, or project management.

## Done criteria

- Final page is mobile-first and browser-checked.
- Final page distinguishes concept visuals from fabrication-accurate CAD/shop drawings.
- Final copy asks for alignment on a focused Visual Strategy Pilot without discussing price.
- Accepted visuals pass `quality-control`.
- `npm run validate` passes.
