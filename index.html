Services

  1. 01

  2. 02

    Avatars, Mascots & Creator Characters

    Build recognizable character identities across face, expression, pose, color, outfit, and a clear presentation set.

  3. 03

    Outfits, Props & Accessories

    Design coordinated costume layers, held props, armor, jewelry, and character add-ons with readable function and material hierarchy.

  4. 04

  5. 05

  6. 06

    Visual Pipeline & Handoff

    Turn references, selected directions, and technical notes into organized checkpoints, presentation pages, and handoff material.

Process

From rough input to a resolved character.

A focused five-stage workflow keeps the artistic decisions connected from the first reference through final presentation.

  1. 01

    Brief & References

    Define the role, audience, use case, references, fixed constraints, deliverables, and what must be decided first.

  2. 02

    Shape the Character

    Develop silhouette, anatomy, proportions, personality, costume, props, and visual identity.

  3. 03

    Build in 3D

    Resolve the major forms, character proportions, costume layers, and supporting elements from broad shapes into a cohesive character.

  4. 04

    Materials & Presentation

    Refine color and material hierarchy, expression, camera, lighting, staging, and the views needed to evaluate the character.

  5. 05

    Final Presentation & Delivery

    Consolidate the selected direction, prepare the agreed presentation views, and organize the final material for the team.

About

3D Character Artist with a painter’s eye and a systems mindset.

Lesly creates expressive 3D characters and the visual systems that keep the work clear from first reference through final presentation.

Her painting background shapes how she approaches color, form, anatomy, silhouette, costume language, and material separation.

Her character work starts with form and identity: a readable silhouette first, followed by anatomy, costume, props, surface contrast, and presentation.

She also builds the structure around the art through reference organization, presentation pages, decision points, and workflow documentation. AI tools support that process when useful, but the character-art judgment remains central.

Beyond the character work, Lesly authored this portfolio website from zero to one: she structured, wrote, and built the site, managed it in Git and GitHub, and deployed it to the cloud.
