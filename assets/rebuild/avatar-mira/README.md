## Reconstruction Priorities

1. Preserve Mira as a stylized young adult: one skull, eye spacing, dark-brown eye color, nose, lips, jaw, ears, neck, and shoulder proportions across every view.
2. Keep the high black ponytail, terracotta wrap, side-swept bangs, loose front strands, hair root, ponytail volume, and rear silhouette consistent.
3. Reconstruct the rust hooded outer layer, black high-neck foundation garment, sleeves, belt, diamond earrings, pendant, and restrained gold hardware as separate readable elements.
4. Match the warm skin, grouped dark hair, woven rust cloth, matte black fabric, and aged-gold response under neutral portrait light.
5. Use the five expressions as shape targets only. Plan brows, eyelids, cheeks, mouth corners, lips, jaw, teeth, tongue, inner mouth, and eye behavior on the matching production model.
6. Capture genuine neutral turns, facial topology, deformation loops, inner-mouth construction, blendshape and viseme tests, rig controls, tracking, target-platform behavior, material setup, source-file context, and exports before adding technical-completion claims.

### Hero

### Identity Views

Create coordinated front, three-quarter, true-profile, and rear upper-body views at one scale on a continuous charcoal studio background. Preserve one face, hair system, hood construction, costume silhouette, jewelry, palette, and material family. Keep the full ponytail and waist-up silhouette visible. Exclude labels, boxes, bubbles, software chrome, topology, and rig controls.

### Expression Direction

Create five coordinated head-and-shoulder views of the same avatar: calm neutral, warm closed-mouth smile, delighted open smile, surprised, and focused. Preserve one adult skull, face, hair, costume, jewelry, and material language. Make eyes, eyelids, brows, cheeks, mouth corners, lips, jaw, teeth, and mouth cavities anatomically plausible. Exclude expression labels, sliders, UI, topology, rig controls, and animation-readiness claims.
